What we believe in

We think all conferences should be free from any form of harassment or discrimination and that everyone should feel welcome and safe in its space.

It's very easy to put something up on our site, but our intention is to actively enforce & live what our code of conduct represents. This is the harder part and we're open to discuss how we can improve.

Physical copies will be available at the event and we'll also cover it in the opening keynote so everyone is aware.

To have a common dialog and understanding, here is our 2016 code of conduct.

About the conference

Brilliant people

Surround yourself with some of the best that South Africa's web fraternity has to offer.

Unleash the best of new technologies

After getting insight into many of the new technologies, you'll be in a good position to leverage these in your next project.

Get your questions answered

Bring your questions and get them answered by peers face-to-face.

Network and mingle

This event will be a fantastic opportunity to make lasting contacts with potential new employees/co-founders/bosses

No longer the domain of script-kiddies and copy & pasters, JavaScript and the community around it has grown up to deliver full-stack solutions that rivals any other platform.
This event brings all the ideas and techniques required to deliver awesomeness together in one room and showcases the power and versatility of JavaScript.

JSinSA is a vendor-neutral conference, so if you're a Ruby, .NET, or Node developer, you'll be at home and surrounded by experts in each of those fields. It's the perfect opportunity to chat with peers in the field and exchange ideas and techniques to broaden your knowledge.

Who should attend?
Anyone keen to learn battle-tested techniques and to see emerging technologies.

The speakers

Are you keen to speak at JSinSA 2016?

We're open to any JavaScript topic from everyone and would really like to have submissions from under-represented groups. There are ways we can help with this, so please get in contact.

Speaker submissions policy

All talk topics are considered, regardless of who sends it.

Typical audience

Practical topics that are relevant to those at the cutting-edge.

The ideal is the attendees can start applying what they've learnt soon after the event.

Content guidelines

Demos are great, too many slides ... not so much.

Talk ideas

Here are some ideas based on current industry changes:

  • Angular 2
  • React Native
  • Aurelia & other frameworks
  • ES 6/2015/2016/Next/"the new JavaScript"
  • TypeScript
  • Ionic & hybrid apps
  • Node
  • npm build/deploy scripts
  • Chakra Core
  • Browser dev tooling
  • JS + robots
  • jQuery 3 ...   :-)

We've experimented with a wider range of topics (i.e. non-JavaScript related)
in the past, but for 2016 we're looking at a 100% JavaScript agenda.

What speakers get

We know how much time and effort goes into preparing a talk and always appreciate it.

Our token of thanks is:

  • invitation to the speakers dinner (partners included)
  • free day pass to the conference
  • special gift (the past few years we have given a gift to the partners)
  • and give you maximum exposure for yourself/brand

We are also investigating other ways of compensation.

How to submit

Please include the following in your submission:

  • title & abstract
  • technical level (easy, medium, hard)
  • time needed to present (incl some buffer for Q&A)
  • please ensure you have read our Code of Conduct

We'll confirm receipt and notify you once we've prepared the agenda.
Send your submissions to simon@brokenkeyboards.com.
For added transparency, please feel free to Tweet about your submission.

And for the record, there is nothing wrong with dialogs that scroll. They're awesome!
